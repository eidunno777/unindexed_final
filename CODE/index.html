ABOUT

The dark net is the portion of the internet purposefully hidden from public view. Almost all actions that take place on these sites are unindexed, allowing the people making them to remain completely anonymous.

In this project I wanted to explore stories that utilized this anonymity in completely different ways. While some will use it to safely expose corruption, or plan non-violent protests, at the same time others will use it to purchase contraband. This is the moral dilemma of the dark net, and like the dark net itself, each of the three stories presented has it’s own moral dilemmas. however, there is much more nuance and ambiguity to the users and the uses of the dark net.

Designed and developed by Eddie Livingstone

Created for a Senior Capstone Project, for the Communication Design Program at the Sam Fox School of Design and Visual Arts, Washington University in St. Louis (2019)

The dark net offers users the power and freedom of complete Internet anonymity. Whether it be using a secure email or buying contraband from a black market, what users choose to do with this freedom is completely up to them. This experience explores three stories that utilize the dark net in significantly different ways.

2003

ANONYMOUS FIRST ORIGINATES ON 4CHAN

4chan is an imageboard website where posts generally have anonymous authors. The concept of Anonymous began as a decentralized community on 4chan that would act anonymously in a coordinated manner, referring to themselves as 'anons'. These coordinated efforts were usually toward a loosely self-agreed goal that was primarily for entertainment purposes (or lulz). In the next 15 years this small and largely immature 4chan community would evolve into an international hacktivist group.

SP - an acronym for suppressive person, a term used in Scientology to describe "antisocial personalities." Many who end up leaving the church are labeled as SPs and members are encouraged to disconnect from them.

2008

PROJECT CHANOLOGY

After a video leaked of Tom Cruise gushing about his total devotion to the doctrines of The Church of Scientology, the Internet and 4chan users in particular had a field day making memes of the video. However, the church began trying to expunge the video from the Internet; sending legal nastygrams to anyone hosting or sharing it. The anons were so enraged by this attempt at censorship that they set out to inflict as much damage as possible on the church. So began Project Chanology, in which the anons wanted not only the lulz, but also for those lulz to have a real-world effect. In dedicating themselves to that latter goal, Anonymous began to develop a real political consciousness.

2010

OPERATION PAYBACK

After WikiLeaks’s release of hundreds of thousands of confidential U.S. documents, MasterCard, PayPal and Visa began blocking payments to the site for alleged terms-of-service violations. The action struck many anons as a transparent attempt to hobble Julian Assange's whistle-blowing organization. So began Operation Payback, in which anons used illegal DDoS attacks to take down the websites of MasterCard and Visa and briefly slow down PayPal to a crawl. In order to avoid jail time, the anons planned and carried out these attacks using the anonymity and protection provided by the dark net.

Anonymous released this video shortly after the attacks. Anonymous almost always releases videos that explain what their motives and who their targets are.

We are anonymous, we are legion, we do not forgive, we do not forget

– ANONYMOUS

2012

FREEDOM OPS

During the Arab Spring, Anonymous provided support to the people fighting to end the oppressive regimes ruling them. This supported consisted of Other Operation Tunisia, Operation Algeria, Operation Egypt, Operation Libya and Operation Syria. In each of these, Anonymous launched DDoS attacks against government websites and provided protestors with anonymizing software such as Tor, allowing people to safety organize resistance and speak out against the regime. The operations helped many of these countries win independence and helped Anonymous gain thousands of new followers from the Middle East.

2019

ANONYMOUS STILL OPERATES WORLDWIDE

Anonymous has evolved from a fringe group of pranksters to an international movement. This evolution could not have occurred without the power and freedom provided by the dark net. The privacy ensured shields the anons, allowing them to plan their attacks and employ illegal tactics to inflict actual damage on those they deems deserving. Without this anonymity, the powerful targets of these attacks would surely punish the perpetrators. For many this would mean jail time, while for others it could even mean death. The dark net allows people to use the web however they choose, without fear of who’s watching; without this anonymity, there is no Anonymous.

END STORY

2006

JULIAN ASSANGE FOUNDS WIKILEAKS

A non-profit website with the purposes of disseminating original documents from anonymous sources and leakers. WikiLeaks utilizes the Tor network making it apart of the dark net, allowing for fully encrypted end-to-end anonymous communication. Thus creating a secure location for whistleblowers to safely expose the corruption of state and corporate power.

It is impossible to correct abuses unless we know that they’re going on.

– JULIAN ASSANGE

2010

WIKILEAKS MAKES MAJOR HEADLINES

After leaking confidential documents from Iceland's Kaupthing Bank, WikiLeaks for its first time got major media coverage. The documents leaked showed that suspiciously large sums of money were loaned to various owners of the bank, and large debts were written off shortly before the collapse of Iceland's banking sector. The leak caused an uproar in Iceland, and ultimately led to four former bosses of the bank bring sentenced to between three and five years in jail

2010

COLLATERAL MURDER

On April 5th, WikiLeaks released a classified U.S. military video depicting the indiscriminate slaying of over a dozen people in the Iraqi suburb of New Baghdad — including two Reuters news staff. WikiLeaks released the full 39-minute video as well as a 17-minute version of the footage, edited down for maximum emotional impact and titled 'Collateral Murder'. Following the video release, the attacks received worldwide coverage and controversy, with both the legality and morality of the attacks, as well as the manipulation of the footage by WikiLeaks coming into question.

2010

WIKILEAKS RELEASES THE LARGEST LEAK OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS IN HISTORY

92,000 documents related to the war in Afghanistan and 400,000 documents related to the Iraq War. The documents revealed a real and uncensored story of both wars, detailing thousands of unreported civilian causalities, evidence of torture as well as other war crimes. Shortly after these leaks, WikiLeaks released 250,000 confidential cables sent among U.S. diplomats, revealing the inner workings of U.S. diplomacy. The U.S. condemned all these leaks, suggested the disclosures put lives at risk and gave adversaries valuable information. As a result, Assange has become targeted by the U.S. government.

REACTIONS FROM U.S.

2019

JULIAN ASSANGE ARRESTED

After spending 7 years in U.K.'s Ecuadorian Embassy, on April 11th Assange was suddenly evicted and arrested by U.K. authorities on behalf of a U.S. extradition warrant. Currently, the U.S. has charged him with one count of conspiracy to commit computer intrusion, but additional charges could be added at a later date. By utilizing the anonymity provided by the dark net, WikiLeaks created the first platform for whistleblowers to safety expose corruption; however, the platform only protects the sources of the leaks not WikiLeaks itself.

END STORY

2010

ROSS ULBRICT BEGINS DEVELOPEMENT ON SILK ROAD

At the age of 26, Ulbrict - a libertarian idealist from Texas - began building the website Silk Road. His vision was to create a free market experiment that had an emphasis on user anonymity. To achieve this, Ulbrict built the site for the dark net, where communication between users would be entirely anonymous. Ulbrict also designed the site to only handle transactions made in bitcoin, which offered a level of anonymity far greater than any other form of currency or credit card. As a result, Silk Road’s existence would be completely outside the scope of government control, allowing users to buy or sell almost anything.

2011

SILK ROAD BECOMES THE BIGGEST DARKNET BLACK MARKET

In its first year live Silk Road took off, quickly becoming the most heavily trafficked dark net black market. Much of its success can be attributed to trustworthiness of the platform. From the start Ulbrict had installed a rating and feedback system into the site, allowing for an online community to emerge and keep dealers in check — thus eliminating much of the fear of buying fake or altered products. Additionally, since purchases were occurring on an online platform, Silk Road eliminated the dangers of meeting with nefarious dealers in person. In essence, Silk Road brought the drug trade into the 21st century.

Ulbrict even created this commercial for the site, which features a hip-hop beat as background music.

People should have the right to buy and sell whatever they wanted so long as they aren't hurting anyone

– ROSS ULBRICT

2012

Despite creating terms of service that were more prohibitive than other dark net black markets, Ulbricht became unwilling or unable to maintain the standards that he had initially set. As a result, as the site evolved, more and more dangerous products, including heroin, guns, and fentanyl, began to be listed. All these listings were easily accessible to everyone of all ages, all you needed was a shipping address and a bitcoin account.

2013

THE FBI SHUTS DOWN SILK ROAD

After almost a two-year long investigation, federal agents apprehended Ulbricht, charging him with narcotics trafficking, computer hacking and money laundering. Ulbrict's undoing came after agents were able to link him back to a username handle which frequently posted about Silk Road in bitcoin forums. In one of these posts the Gmail account 'rossulbricht@gmail.com,' was included. As part of the investigation, it was determined that over $1.2 billion in sales had occurred on Silk Road, which had acquired nearly a million users worldwide. On February 4, 2015, Ulbrict was convicted on all non-violent counts and sentenced to double life imprisonment plus forty years, without the possibility of parole.

Rather than end the online black market trade, the shutdown of Silk Road simply caused other dark net black market sites to spring up to take over the demand left behind. Today there are many black markets still on the dark net, many of which are much larger than the Silk Road, and contain no terms-of-service what so ever. When a one of these dark markets is taken down, people go to the next one. Making one thing very clear: as long as users can remain anonymous and money can be made, a black market will always exist on the dark net.
